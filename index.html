About Fred

I received my B.S. in Physics with a concentration in Astrophysics at New Mexico Tech in 2010, and received a Ph.D. in Astronomy from UCLA in 2015. Since Fall 2019 I have been a postdoctoral fellow in the Computational Research Division of Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.

Check the tabs above (most importantly, the Research one) to see what I'm all about. In brief, I primarily study the ionizing background in the intergalactic medium at high redshift with analytic models and large-scale semi-numerical simulations, along with more detailed ionizing radiative transfer around quasars during (and after) reionization.

(Unrelated to my primary research interests, I am also involved in a 7 mm/43 GHz VLBA monitoring campaign of the M87 radio jet, specifically focusing on phase-referenced micro-arcsecond-precision astrometry.)
